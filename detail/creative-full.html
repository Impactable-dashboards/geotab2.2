Read this first, two measurement rules. (1) CTR is not comparable across objectives. Engagement-objective campaigns (Document/ENG) report "clicks" that are document expands, not website visits: they post a 5.18% CTR on $30K of spend but only 2 site conversions. Website-Visit campaigns are the comparable set (benchmark CTR 0.183%). (2) Conversions are sparse (81 total) so CPA is an efficiency signal to validate at scale, not a precise cost. This read classifies every campaign by format, angle, funnel stage, and objective from the campaign taxonomy; literal ad copy sits one level deeper than this view. The single clearest reallocation

The two most efficient formats are the two least funded.

Spotlight and Text together drove 33 of 81 conversions (41%) on $9,502 of spend, a blended CPA near $290. Image and Document together absorbed $138,115 (74% of NA spend) for 34 conversions, with Document delivering near-zero pipeline. Shifting even 15–20% of Image/Document budget into Spotlight and Text is the highest-confidence efficiency move in the account.

Objective integrity & funnel shape

Critical · measurement

$30K of engagement spend, 2 conversions.

Engagement-objective Document ads post a 5.18% CTR (28x the website-visit benchmark) because the "clicks" are doc expands. They generate the dashboard's biggest click numbers and almost no pipeline. Report these on engagement, never blended into CTR, and question the $29,940 allocation.

Watch · funnel

Bottom-funnel is under-funded for how it converts.

BOF is only $24,323 (13% of spend) but produced 18 conversions at the best efficiency in the account (Spotlight Request-Demo at $106 CPA). TOF absorbs $87,099. There is room to move spend down-funnel where intent and conversion are highest.

Opportunity · angle

Transport & Logistics is the messaging winner.

T&L is the largest angle ($28,941) AND the conversion leader (20 conv, $1,447 CPA). Retargeting/warm audiences convert cheapest (17 conv at $457) and Case Studies content punches above weight (7 conv at $980). Concentrate message + budget here.

Critical · strategy

The 7-Play persona system is running on fumes.

The five named-persona ABM campaigns (Safety, Operations, Routing, Samsara, Deal Accel) carry tiny budgets (each under $700) and produced near-zero clicks and 0 conversions YTD. The client-facing framework is effectively unfunded. Decision needed: fund it to a testable level or fold it into the vertical structure that is working.

The biggest creative opportunity

Move the personas onto the formats that convert.

The persona angles are strategically right but executed in starved, low-converting setups. The account already proves Spotlight and Text convert at $221–$369. Rebuilding the five personas as Spotlight/Text BOF-MOF motions (instead of the current near-zero campaigns) is the path to making the 7-Play system actually produce pipeline.
