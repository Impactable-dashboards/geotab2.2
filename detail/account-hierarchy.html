The structural headline

Across all regions, 68% of spend sits in Top of Funnel and only 6% in Bottom of Funnel. The portfolio is built to reach, not to close. Bottom-funnel exists in only two accounts (EMEA's 1:1 RFP motion and NA's single demo campaign); LATAM and APAC have no BOF layer at all. This is the budget-to-efficiency inversion the channel diagnostic flagged, now visible structurally.

NA — the orchestrated core

Heaviest, most balanced account ($185,885, 81 conv). Strong TOF + MOF retargeting. Its account-based motion is the 7-play persona system, named by persona rather than "ABM," so it sits inside TOF/MOF here rather than a labeled BOF tier.

EMEA — more full-funnel than assumed

The only account with a real Bottom-funnel layer: $20,169 across ~20 named 1:1 RFP campaigns (Vinci, Philips, Holcim, EDF, Veolia, KONE, Biffa). Corrects the overview's "EMEA = top + middle only." Also carries 32 dormant/legacy campaigns to clean up.

LATAM — lean and cold

9 campaigns, ~$29,232, almost entirely cold TOFU (image, carousel, GO Focus). No retargeting depth, no BOF. Efficient per-click but no funnel to convert into.

APAC — reach-heavy, thin engagement

$23,320 on mostly TOFU reach. Half its spend ($11,161) sits in 3 "Cloud ABM" campaigns that delivered very low volume. Consistent with APAC's account-level reach-without-engagement finding.
