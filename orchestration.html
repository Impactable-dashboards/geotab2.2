Strategy spine · the centerpiece

The orchestration model

The strategy that ties every surface together. The execution is already strong across channels and markets. What is missing is the layer that connects them, qualifies demand before it reaches sales, and measures the whole thing against pipeline. This is the path from fragmented execution to orchestrated, measured demand.

The channel-role map
● The hub

The orchestration & measurement layer

The hub is not any single channel, it is the orchestration and measurement layer that ties them together: search captures in-market demand, LinkedIn reads and qualifies the buying-committee signal, programmatic extends reach, and the always-on layer holds every signal until it is ready to route to the field. This is where fragmented execution becomes one orchestrated motion.

In-market capture · Geotab in-house

Search

Geotab runs paid search (Google and Microsoft) in-house, catching active, in-market demand. The intent is real and high, but today it is captured and then dropped, not handed to the orchestration layer.

Open-web reach

Programmatic

StackAdapt extends reach across the open web and de-risks single-channel dependency. Efficient at the top, ready to carry the always-on retargeting layer.

Signal generators

Organic, SEO, page, email

Company page, SEO, and Marketo email are mature but siloed. Each one generates signal the orchestration layer should be reading, not losing.

The loop to close

Reseller

The reseller hand-off is where attribution goes dark today. Closing this loop is what makes influenced pipeline visible end to end.

The anchor · un-retargeted intent
● The evidence the rebalance turns on

Healthy paid search, generating intent we are not yet catching.

Geotab runs healthy paid search in North America, on our read $50K or more per month, and it generates exactly the in-market intent the program wants. Today that intent is captured at the click and then goes un-nurtured, with no always-on layer holding it. The clearest single case sits in the spine: Rogers Communications was a top LinkedIn engager, then went to zero, while in the same window 6sense flagged it as the single biggest surge at 232 web visits. The demand did not disappear. It fell through the gap between channels.

◆ Immature

Orchestration & measurement

Channels run in isolation, intent falls between them, and attribution stops before the CRM. This is the gap, and it is where the upside sits.

→ Destination

Measured demand

One connected system, demand qualified before sales, measured against pipeline. Reached in tiers: the named-account spine today, connected measurement next, person-level depth on the pilot.

The asks
1

Rebalance the funnel

Shift weight from awareness-heavy toward the mid and bottom layers already converting most efficiently, and give warm high-fit accounts a clear owner so none fall between segments.

2

Stand up the always-on retargeting layer

An organization-level retargeting layer that holds the search and web intent instead of letting it fall through, like the Rogers case. No new budget to start, it runs on what is already in market.

Catches the in-market search intent already being paid for
3

Connect DemandSense to Salesforce

Close the loop from advertising into the CRM so influenced pipeline is finally visible end to end, including the reseller hand-off that goes dark today.

4

Pilot WebID

Add person-level resolution to the highest-intent accounts as a focused pilot: sharper measurement, deeper ABM intelligence, and named, ready-to-contact fuel for the field.

5

Bring search under one orchestration

Pull paid search into the connected layer so capture and influence finally talk to each other.
