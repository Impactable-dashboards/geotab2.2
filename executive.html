01 The Picture Today

A sophisticated, multichannel engine, assembled in one place for the first time.

Geotab runs an enterprise-grade demand stack across four global markets and every major channel. The strength is real and the execution is sophisticated. The pattern that matters for the next chapter: these channels are firing independently, each measured on its own terms, with no single layer connecting awareness to qualification to pipeline.

The opportunity is not to add more. It is to connect what already works into one orchestrated, measurable system, with LinkedIn as the layer that qualifies demand and feeds your sales team the accounts that are ready.

03 The Opportunity

The pieces are strong. The connective tissue is the upside.

None of this is a performance problem. It is an orchestration and measurement opportunity, the kind that compounds the return on spend you are already making rather than asking for more of it.

● Strengths to scale

  • ABM motion. full-funnel, signal-led, multi-persona, feeding sales. The blueprint.
  • Programmatic reach. efficient open-web extension, actively de-risking single-channel dependency.
  • Mid-funnel efficiency. strong engagement on lean budget, ready to replicate into other segments and markets.
  • Weekly sales-intel loop. intent surges and competitive signals delivered to reps, already in the workflow.

◆ Where the upside sits

  • Un-retargeted search intent. NA paid search is running healthy, on our read $50K or more per month, generating in-market intent that is not yet retargeted or nurtured. The single clearest rebalance signal.
  • Channels run disconnected. efficient capture (Google, Meta, Programmatic) does not yet talk to the LinkedIn influence layer or to each other.
  • Measurement stops at the channel. no closed-loop tie-back to CRM outside ABM; the reseller hand-off is an attribution blind spot.
  • Coverage built segment-first. strong niches, but warm high-fit accounts can fall between segments without a clear owner.
  • Spend skews to awareness. program-wide, the funnel weights heavily to the top, with room to rebalance toward conversion.

05 The Path Forward

Three moves. Each one compounds the spend you already make.

A sequenced plan, each step framed by what it returns to Geotab, not what it adds to a scope. The goal is more pipeline per dollar already in market.

1

Rebalance the funnel and catch the search intent

Shift program weight from awareness-heavy toward the mid and bottom layers already converting most efficiently, and give warm high-fit accounts a clear owner so none fall between segments. The proof sits in plain view: NA paid search is running at $50K or more per month, and that in-market intent is not yet retargeted. Stand up an always-on, org-level retargeting layer to hold it, and bring search under one orchestration, with no new budget to start.

What it unlocksMore qualified demand and conversion from spend already in market, starting with the $600K or more a year of search intent that currently goes un-nurtured.
2

Connect the measurement layer

Stand up closed-loop attribution from advertising into Salesforce, using our measurement layer (DemandSense) plus offline conversion sync, and close the reseller hand-off blind spot so influenced pipeline is finally visible end to end.

What it unlocksOne source of truth for what marketing is actually influencing, the exact investment-by-channel view leadership has asked for.
3

Pilot person-level resolution (WebID)

Add person-level identity to the highest-intent accounts as a focused pilot. Three capabilities in one: sharper measurement, deeper ABM intelligence, and named, ready-to-contact fuel for the sales team, including the high-engagement accounts not yet owned.

What it unlocksAccounts already raising their hand, like AT&T and Verizon, routed to a rep before a competitor gets there.
